7. Touch-aware morph: dot → compact switch try it

Visible part is the 10 × 10 dot, anchored to the table's upper-right corner. Transparent padding extends the hit target into the table to make it ≥ 24 × 24 even when collapsed.
Mouse: hover-grow into a 28 × 16 compact switch; mouse-out shrinks it back. Pure CSS via @media (hover: hover) and (pointer: fine).
Touch: first tap expands; second tap toggles state; auto-collapses after 3 s of no interaction, or when you tap outside. Detected via e.pointerType at pointerdown.
Keyboard: Tab to focus expands it (:focus-visible); Space/Enter toggles.

The current extension anchors the control's right edge to the table's right edge and lifts it above the table top. Drag the sliders to see every control move together. Note the practical floor: accessibility guidance puts the minimum tap target around 24 px, so visuals smaller than that need invisible padding — which adds the footprint back. #7 expands on hover/focus; on touch there is no hover, so it would need a tap-to-expand fallback.
